retrieve

retrieve sth.(from sb.)

formal

  1. to bring or grt sth back, expecially from a place where it should not be
    (rethieve my money from the floor)
  2. to find or get back data or information that has beeb stored in the memory of a computer(rethieve items quickly)
  3. to make a bad situation better; to get back sth that has lost
    (You can only rethieve the situation by apologizing. )

retriever n.寻回犬

erase

  1. erase sth（from sth）;to remove sth completely(sth tried to erase the memory of that evening;all doults were suddenly erased from his mind)
  2. to make a mark or sth you have wtitten disappear, for example dy rubbing it, expecially in order to correct it(he had erased the wrong word)
  3. to remove a recording from a tapr or information from a computer’s memory

susceptible

  1. [not usually before noun]~(to sb/sth);very likely to be influenced, harmed or affected by sb/sth(he’s highly susceptible to flattery恭维话；some of the plants are more susceptible to frost damage then others;salt intake may lead to raised blood pressure in susceptible aldults)
  2. easily influened by feelings and emotions(同：impressionable)(She was both charming and susceptible她迷人而多情)
  3. susceptible of sth.

formal

allowing sth; capable of sth;(is this situation not susceptible of improvement by legislation?)

modification

modification of/to/in sth.

the act or process of changing sth in order to improve it or make it more acceptable; a change that is made(同：adaptation)

Considerable modification of the existing system is need.

It maight be necessary to make a few slight modifications to be design.

eradicate

eradicate sth from sth

to destory or get rid of sth completely, especially sth bad(wipe out)

Polio has been virtually eradicated in Brazil.

We are determined to eradicate racism from our sport.

cognitive

usually before noun

connected with mental processes of understanding

认知的，感知的，认识的

a child’s cognitive development

cognitive psychology

disorder

n.混乱，紊乱，疾病

the disorder in

in disorder

bipolar disorder双向型障碍

v.使混乱

condition

  1. [un]状态in (bad/good) condition

  2. [un]健康状况out of condition;in a critical condition

  3. [cn]疾病a medical condition; a serious heart condition

  4. [复数名词]（居住、工作、或做事情的）环境、境况、条件living/housing/working condions;changing economic conditions; a strike to improve pay and conditions

  5. [复数名词]影响某事件发生的物质环境，状态，条件the plants grow in cool, damp conditions; freezing conditions;condions are ideal for…

  6. [cn]条件，条款the terms and the conditions of employment;the offer is subject to certain conditions; on condion that;

  7. [cn]先决条件、前提 a necessary condition for economic growth

  8. [单数名词]（formal）某群体的生存状态，处境he spoke angrily about the condition of the urban poor

  9. on no condition

  10. in mint condition完美无缺

procedure

n.程序，手续，步骤

stimulus

pl.stimuli

n.（使生物产生反应的）刺激，刺激物，促进因素，激励因素

stimulate

be a stimulus to

therapy

n. 治疗

excessive

adj.过分的过度的

trauma

n. 创伤，外伤，损伤，精神创伤，痛苦经历，挫折

he confronted his childhood trauma and tried to exorcise the pain

victim

n.受害者，罹难者，牺牲品，受骗者，牺牲（故意）祭品

stop regarding yours as the victim

verdict

n.判决，陪审团的裁定，裁断，经过检验或认真考虑的决定意见结论

the board has been slow to render its verdict.

pronounce their verdicts

decent

adj.得体的，像样的，正派的，相当不错的，合乎礼节的

circumstance

n. 条件，环境，状况，境遇

vt.使处于某种特定的情况之下

conduct

n.行为，举止；经营方式；管理方法

v.实施；执行；引导；组织；安排；带领

exceed

vt.超越（数量，法律）

it is dangerous to exceed the recommended dose.

infect

vt.传染，使携带病菌，使感染计算机病毒；使感染某种感情
